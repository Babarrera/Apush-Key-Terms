🔴 PERIOD 1: 1491–1607 — The Meeting of Three Worlds

3 C's: Contact, Commerce, Competing philosophies.

1. Native Societies Before European Contact

Diverse, complex societies adapted to environments. Aztecs (Tenochtitlan, tribute), Pueblos (maize, cliff dwellings), Eastern Woodlands (matrilineal Iroquois). Maize → population growth, permanent villages. Cahokia (30,000). Animism, Iroquois Confederacy.

2. Columbus’s Voyage (1492) → Columbian Exchange

Global transfer of goods, diseases, people. Natives had no immunity → 80% population decline. Horses, cattle, wheat to New World; corn, potatoes, tobacco to Old World. Transatlantic slavery rises.

3. Competing Philosophies

Doctrine of Discovery (1493). Valladolid Debates: Sepúlveda (“natural slaves”) vs. Las Casas (Indians have souls, capacity for reason).

4. Spanish Colonization: Encomienda, Mission System, Caste System (Peninsulares, Creoles, Mestizos). Pueblo Revolt (1680) – successful uprising.

✅ Period 1 Key Takeaways: Natives diverse; Columbian Exchange transformed world; competing philosophies shaped colonization.

🟠 PERIOD 2: 1607–1754 — The Crisis of Empire

3 G’s: Gold, God, Glory. 7 themes: Identity, Work/tech, Peopling, Politics/power, America & world, Environment, Ideas/belief.

First Great Awakening (1730s–1770s)

Key figures: Jonathan Edwards, George Whitefield. Challenged traditional authority; fostered colonial identity. Effects: undermined established churches, individualism.

Metacom’s War (1675–76) & Bacon’s Rebellion (1676)

Bacon’s Rebellion: land scarcity, conflict with Natives → planters shifted to African slavery. Metacom’s War: bloody conflict devastated Native power in New England.

Enlightenment & Salutary Neglect

Enlightenment (Locke, natural rights) provided revolutionary foundation. Salutary neglect led to colonial autonomy.

🟡 PERIOD 3: 1754–1800 — Conflict, Nation Building & Expansion

French and Indian War (1754–1763)

Britain vs France over Ohio Valley. Treaty of Paris 1763: Britain gained Canada & land east of Mississippi. Effect: British debt → end of salutary neglect → new taxes, Proclamation of 1763, Pontiac’s Rebellion.

Revolutionary War & Declaration of Independence (1776)

Common Sense (Paine). French alliance after Saratoga (1777). Yorktown (1781). Treaty of Paris 1783.

Articles of Confederation (1781–1789)

Weaknesses: no tax, no executive, 9/13 for laws. Accomplishments: Northwest Ordinance (1787) banned slavery in NW territory, set statehood process.
Shays’ Rebellion (1786–87): exposed weakness → Constitutional Convention.

Constitution & Compromises

Great Compromise (House by population, Senate equal). 3/5 Compromise. Federalists vs Anti-Federalists → Bill of Rights (1791).

Washington & Adams presidencies

Hamilton’s Financial Plan (national bank, assumption, whiskey tax). Whiskey Rebellion (1794) shows federal power. Alien & Sedition Acts (1798). Kentucky & Virginia Resolutions (nullification).
Revolution of 1800: peaceful transfer of power (Jefferson beats Adams).

DBQ guide: 25% exam score; thesis, contextualization, evidence (4+ docs), HIPP analysis.

🟢 PERIOD 4: 1800–1848 — What does it mean to be American?

Louisiana Purchase (1803) – doubled US size.

Marbury v. Madison (1803): judicial review. War of 1812 → increased nationalism, ended Federalist Party. Monroe Doctrine (1823): warned Europe away from W. Hemisphere.

Market Revolution & Reform

Erie Canal, railroads, telegraph, interchangeable parts, cotton gin. Lowell Mill girls. Irish & German immigration → nativism (Know‑Nothing Party).
Second Great Awakening (Finney) → Temperance, Abolition (Garrison, Douglass), Women’s Rights (Seneca Falls 1848 – Declaration of Sentiments).

Jacksonian Era

Expansion of democracy (white male suffrage). Indian Removal Act (1830) → Trail of Tears (4,000 Cherokee died). Nullification Crisis (SC vs. tariff). Bank War → Panic of 1837.
Missouri Compromise (1820): 36°30' line, ME free, MO slave. Temporary solution.

Key Court Cases & Laws

McCulloch v. Maryland (1819) – implied powers, BUS constitutional. Gibbons v. Ogden (1824) – interstate commerce. Worcester v. Georgia (1832) – Jackson ignored.

🔵 PERIOD 5: 1844–1877 — Expansion → Crisis → Rebuilding

Manifest Destiny & Mexican-American War (1846–1848)

Texas annexed (1845). Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo → Mexican Cession. Wilmot Proviso (failed). Compromise of 1850 (Fugitive Slave Law). Kansas‑Nebraska Act (1854) → Bleeding Kansas, Republican Party formed.

Road to Civil War

Dred Scott v. Sandford (1857): Blacks not citizens, Missouri Compromise unconstitutional. Lincoln‑Douglas debates (1858). John Brown’s raid (1859). Election of 1860 → secession.

Civil War (1861–1865)

Emancipation Proclamation (1863) freed slaves in rebel areas. Gettysburg & Vicksburg turned tide. Sherman’s March. Appomattox (1865). 13th Amendment (abolished slavery).

Reconstruction (1865–1877)

14th Amendment (citizenship, equal protection), 15th Amendment (voting rights). Black Codes, sharecropping, KKK violence. Compromise of 1877 ends Reconstruction → Jim Crow, Plessy v. Ferguson (1896) “separate but equal.”

🟣 PERIOD 6: 1865–1898 — The Gilded Age

Industrial Titans & Labor

Carnegie (vertical integration, Gospel of Wealth), Rockefeller (horizontal integration, Standard Oil). Knights of Labor → Haymarket Riot (1886). AFL (Gompers). Homestead (1892) & Pullman (1894) strikes crushed by government.

West & Native Americans

Dawes Act (1887) broke up tribal lands. Wounded Knee (1890). Chinese Exclusion Act (1882).

Populist Party (Omaha Platform 1892)

Free silver, direct election of senators, income tax, gov ownership of railroads. Election of 1896: Bryan “Cross of Gold” loses to McKinley. Progressive Era later adopts many reforms.

Plessy v. Ferguson (1896)

“Separate but equal” legalizes Jim Crow. Ida B. Wells anti‑lynching; Booker T. Washington vs. W.E.B. Du Bois.

⚪ PERIOD 7: 1890–1945 — Imperialism · Progressive Era · WWI · 20s · Great Depression · WWII

Imperialism (1890–1920)

Spanish‑American War (1898) → US gains Philippines, Guam, Puerto Rico. Platt Amendment (Cuba). Open Door Policy. Roosevelt Corollary (1904).

Progressive Era

Muckrakers (Sinclair, Tarbell). 16th (income tax), 17th (direct election of senators), 18th (Prohibition), 19th (women’s suffrage). Square Deal (TR), New Freedom (Wilson). Federal Reserve Act (1913), Clayton Antitrust Act.

World War I & Home Front

Lusitania, Zimmermann Telegram. Espionage/Sedition Acts, Schenck v. US (clear & present danger). Great Migration. Wilson’s 14 Points; Treaty of Versailles rejected by Senate.

1920s & Great Depression

Harlem Renaissance, Scopes Trial, Red Scare, Quota Acts. Black Tuesday (1929), Hoover’s failure → New Deal (FDR, 1933–1938): FDIC, SEC, CCC, WPA, Social Security, Wagner Act. Did not end Depression (WWII did).

World War II

Pearl Harbor (Dec 7, 1941). Japanese Internment (Korematsu v. US). Double V Campaign, Rosie the Riveter. D-Day, atomic bombs (Hiroshima, Nagasaki) – Japan surrenders Aug 1945. US joins United Nations.

📇 FLASHCARDS — Timeline order (Periods 1–7)

Each card includes: Name | Definition/Details | Causes | Effects – all preserved from original teacher materials.

1. Pre-Columbian Native Societies
1491–1607
Definition: Diverse, complex societies. Aztecs (Tenochtitlan), Pueblos (maize), Eastern Woodlands (Iroquois). Maize led to population growth. Cahokia 30,000.
Causes: Migration via Bering Strait; adaptation to environments.
Effects: Population density; complex political structures; vulnerability to European diseases.
2. Columbus’s Voyage (1492)
Causes: European search for direct trade routes to Asia.
Effects: Columbian Exchange; Native demographic catastrophe (80%+ died); transatlantic slave trade.
3. Columbian Exchange
Causes: Contact between hemispheres after 1492.
Effects: Horses, cattle to New World; corn, potatoes to Old World; smallpox kills millions; tobacco fuels plantation slavery; racial caste systems.
4. Valladolid Debates (1550–1551)
Causes: Spanish brutality; religious/moral questions.
Effects: Shifted some Spanish policies; precedent for human rights discussions.
5. Pueblo Revolt (1680)
Causes: Encomienda, forced conversion.
Effects: Natives drove Spanish out for ~12 years; Spanish later more tolerant.
6. Jamestown (1607) & First Africans (1619)
Causes: Joint-stock company; labor demand.
Effects: Tobacco colony; Bacon’s Rebellion → shift to African slavery; House of Burgesses.
7. Bacon’s Rebellion (1676)
Causes: Land scarcity for freed indentured servants; resentment toward elite.
Effects: Planters turned to African slaves; exposed class tensions; accelerated race-based slavery.
8. First Great Awakening (1730s–1770s)
Causes: Reaction against dry Enlightenment rationalism; desire for emotional faith.
Effects: Undermined traditional authority; encouraged individualism; fostered shared colonial identity.
9. French and Indian War (1754–1763)
Causes: Ohio River Valley dispute.
Effects: British debt → end of salutary neglect → new taxes; Proclamation of 1763; Pontiac’s Rebellion.
10. Stamp Act (1765) & Intolerable Acts (1774)
Causes: British need for revenue after war debt.
Effects: Stamp Act Congress, Sons of Liberty, boycotts; First Continental Congress.
11. Declaration of Independence (1776)
Causes: Enlightenment ideas (Locke), British tyranny.
Effects: Formalized revolution; inspired other movements.
12. Articles of Confederation (1781) & Shays’ Rebellion (1786–87)
Causes: Fear of strong central government; post-war depression.
Effects: Exposed weakness → Constitutional Convention (1787).
13. Constitution & Great Compromise (1787)
Causes: AOC failed; need for stronger national government.
Effects: House by population, Senate equal; 3/5 Compromise; Bill of Rights (1791).
14. Louisiana Purchase (1803) / Marbury v. Madison (1803)
Causes: Napoleon needed money; Jefferson wanted New Orleans.
Effects: Doubled US size; judicial review established.
15. Missouri Compromise (1820)
Causes: Dispute over slavery in Louisiana Purchase.
Effects: Temporarily resolved sectional conflict; 36°30' line.
16. Indian Removal Act (1830) & Trail of Tears (1838)
Causes: Desire for southern land (gold); Jackson’s hostility.
Effects: Cherokee forced to Oklahoma (~4,000 died); Worcester v. Georgia ignored.
17. Seneca Falls Convention (1848)
Causes: Women’s experience in abolitionist movement.
Effects: Declaration of Sentiments; women’s suffrage movement launched.
18. Kansas-Nebraska Act (1854) & Bleeding Kansas
Causes: Stephen Douglas wanted transcontinental railroad; popular sovereignty.
Effects: Destroyed Whig Party; created Republican Party; violent conflict (“Bleeding Kansas”).
19. Dred Scott v. Sandford (1857)
Causes: Dred Scott sued for freedom; Taney Court pro-slavery.
Effects: Declared Missouri Compromise unconstitutional; Blacks not citizens; pushed nation toward war.
20. Emancipation Proclamation (1863)
Causes: Military necessity; keep Europe from aiding Confederacy.
Effects: Freed slaves in rebel areas; allowed Black enlistment (180,000+).
21. Reconstruction Amendments (13th, 14th, 15th)
Causes: Need to integrate freed people and protect rights.
Effects: Abolished slavery, citizenship, voting rights (but Jim Crow later nullified).
22. Compromise of 1877
Causes: Disputed 1876 election.
Effects: Ended Reconstruction; federal troops withdrawn; Jim Crow segregation begins.
23. Chinese Exclusion Act (1882) & Dawes Act (1887)
Causes: Nativism, fear of job competition; desire to assimilate Natives.
Effects: First race‑based immigration ban; loss of 90 million acres of tribal land.
24. Populist Party (1892) & Election of 1896
Causes: Falling crop prices, railroad monopolies, debt.
Effects: Omaha Platform (free silver, direct election, income tax). Populist dissolved but reforms later adopted.
25. Spanish-American War (1898)
Causes: Yellow journalism, De Lôme Letter, USS Maine explosion.
Effects: US got Guam, Puerto Rico, Philippines; emerged as world power; Platt Amendment made Cuba a protectorate.
26. Progressive Era Amendments (16th, 17th, 18th, 19th)
Causes: Progressive reform; women’s suffrage campaigns; temperance movement.
Effects: Income tax, direct election of senators, prohibition (later repealed), women’s voting rights.
27. U.S. Entry into WWI (1917)
Causes: Unrestricted submarine warfare, Zimmermann Telegram.
Effects: U.S. tipped balance to Allies; Wilson’s 14 Points; Senate rejected Treaty of Versailles.
28. Great Migration & Harlem Renaissance
Causes: Escape Jim Crow, racial violence; industrial jobs in North.
Effects: Black cultural flowering; race riots; foundation for civil rights movement.
29. Black Tuesday (1929) & New Deal (1933–1938)
Causes: Stock speculation, buying on margin, overproduction.
Effects: FDIC, SEC, Social Security, Wagner Act; modern safety net created; WWII ends Depression.
30. Pearl Harbor (1941) & WWII home front
Causes: Japanese expansion, U.S. embargo.
Effects: U.S. enters war; Japanese Internment (Korematsu); Rosie the Riveter; Double V Campaign; atomic bombs end war in 1945.
