🔴 PERIOD 1 (1491–1607)
1. Pre-Columbian Native Societies
📖 Definition / Details
Diverse, complex societies adapted to environments. Examples: Aztecs (urban Tenochtitlan, tribute), Pueblos (maize, cliff dwellings), Eastern Woodlands (matrilineal Iroquois, hunting/farming mix). Cahokia (30,000 people near St. Louis, 1200 CE). Maize cultivation led to less hunting/gathering, permanent villages. Iroquois Confederacy. Animism.
⚡ Causes
Migration via Bering Strait; adaptation to regional environments.
📌 Effects
Population growth (Tenochtitlan 250,000); complex political structures; vulnerability to European diseases later.
🔴 PERIOD 1
2. Columbus's Voyage (1492)
📖 Definition
Triggered Columbian Exchange – global transfer of goods, people, diseases, and ideas between Old and New Worlds.
⚡ Causes
European search for direct trade routes to Asia; Spanish sponsorship of Columbus.
📌 Effects
Demographic catastrophe for Natives (80%+ died from disease); rise of transatlantic slave trade; European dominance in Americas.
🔴 PERIOD 1
3. Columbian Exchange
📖 Definition
Widespread exchange of animals, plants, microbes, and people. Specifics: Horses transformed Native mobility; corn/potatoes → European population boom; smallpox killed millions; tobacco fueled plantation economies; slavery created racial caste systems.
⚡ Causes
Contact between previously separate hemispheres after 1492.
📌 Effects
New World got horses, cattle, wheat, sugar, and Old World diseases. Old World got corn, potatoes, tomatoes, tobacco, syphilis. Massive Native depopulation; African slavery expanded.
🔴 PERIOD 1
4. Valladolid Debates (1550–1551)
📖 Definition
Sepúlveda argued Indians are "natural slaves" – intellectually inferior, savage practices, no private property. Las Casas argued Indians are brothers – sophisticated societies, capacity for reason, share humanity and Christian brotherhood.
⚡ Causes
Spanish brutality and forced conversion of Natives; religious and moral questions about conquest.
📌 Effects
No clear winner, but Las Casas helped shift some Spanish policies; debate set precedent for human rights discussions.
🔴 PERIOD 1
5. Pueblo Revolt (1680)
📖 Definition
Successful Native uprising against Spanish in present-day New Mexico. Destroyed churches, killed settlers, drove Spanish out for ~12 years.
⚡ Causes
Forced labor (encomienda), forced conversion, suppression of Pueblo religion.
📌 Effects
Spanish returned with more tolerance; became more cautious about forced conversion; symbol of Native resistance.
🔴 PERIOD 1
6. Encomienda System
📖 Definition
Forced Native labor on plantations and mines. Spanish crown granted colonists the right to extract labor/tribute from Natives in exchange for "protection" and Christian instruction.
⚡ Causes
Need for cheap labor after Columbus; Spanish desire to extract gold/silver.
📌 Effects
Massive Native population decline; replaced by African slave labor as Natives died; creation of mestizo/mulatto classes.
🟠 PERIOD 2 (1607–1754)
7. Jamestown Founded (1607)
📖 Definition
First permanent English settlement in North America. Initially disastrous (disease, starvation, conflict). Tobacco saved the colony.
⚡ Causes
Joint-stock company (Virginia Company) seeking profit; English rivalry with Spain.
📌 Effects
Headright system to attract labor; Bacon's Rebellion (1676) → shift from indentured servants to African slavery; House of Burgesses (1619) – first representative assembly.
🟠 PERIOD 2
8. First Africans in Jamestown (1619)
📖 Definition
First recorded Africans arrive in English North America. Initially treated as indentured servants, but set stage for race-based chattel slavery.
⚡ Causes
Atlantic slave trade already established; demand for labor in tobacco fields.
📌 Effects
Gradual codification of chattel slavery (status of mother determined enslavement); rigid racial caste system.
🟠 PERIOD 2
9. Mayflower Compact (1620)
📖 Definition
Agreement signed by Pilgrims before landing at Plymouth. Established self-government based on majority rule. First written framework of government in English colony.
⚡ Causes
Pilgrims landed outside Virginia Company's jurisdiction; needed social contract to maintain order.
📌 Effects
Model for later democratic governance; influenced New England town meetings.
🟠 PERIOD 2
10. Massachusetts Bay / "City upon a Hill" (1630)
📖 Definition
Puritan colony led by John Winthrop. "City upon a hill" – model Christian society. Self-governance through freemen voting.
⚡ Causes
Religious persecution in England; desire to create reformed Protestant society.
📌 Effects
Religious intolerance (banished Roger Williams, Anne Hutchinson); town meeting government; foundation of New England identity.
🟠 PERIOD 2
11. New Amsterdam (1624–1664)
📖 Definition
Dutch trading hub (later New York). Joint-stock company, religious toleration, fur trade alliances with Iroquois (guns).
⚡ Causes
Dutch desire for commercial profit; mercantilism.
📌 Effects
Became New York after English takeover (1664); legacy of religious and ethnic diversity.
🟠 PERIOD 2
12. New France (1608)
📖 Definition
French colony centered on fur trade. Alliances and intermarriage with Natives (Métis). Few settlers, no representative assembly.
⚡ Causes
Fur trade (beaver pelts) highly profitable; French Catholic missions.
📌 Effects
Allied with Huron against Iroquois; limited colonial population; lost to Britain in 1763.
🟠 PERIOD 2
13. Bacon's Rebellion (1676)
📖 Definition
Led by Nathaniel Bacon – frontier farmers (former indentured servants) attacked Native Americans and then burned Jamestown, opposing Governor Berkeley's friendly Native policies.
⚡ Causes
Land scarcity for freed indentured servants; conflict with Natives on frontier; resentment toward coastal elite.
📌 Effects
Planters turned to African slaves as more controllable labor force. Exposed class tensions. Accelerated shift to race-based slavery.
🟠 PERIOD 2
14. Metacom's War / King Philip's War (1675–76)
📖 Definition
Bloody conflict between New England colonists and Native coalition led by Metacom (King Philip). Devastated Native power in New England.
⚡ Causes
Colonial expansion onto Native lands; broken treaties; colonists' pressure on Native resources.
📌 Effects
Thousands dead on both sides; Native resistance crushed; New England colonists secured dominance; symbolized failure of coexistence.
🟠 PERIOD 2
15. Salem Witch Trials (1692)
📖 Definition
Series of hearings and executions in colonial Massachusetts for alleged witchcraft. 20+ executed. Example of religious extremism and social anxiety.
⚡ Causes
Puritan religious intensity; social tensions (family feuds, economic changes); fear of outsiders/change.
📌 Effects
Decline of Puritan religious authority; shift toward more rational/Enlightenment thinking; caution against mass hysteria.
🟠 PERIOD 2
16. Stono Rebellion (1739)
📖 Definition
Slave uprising in South Carolina. ~20 enslaved people gathered near Stono River, killed several whites, attempted to flee to Spanish Florida.
⚡ Causes
Brutal conditions of chattel slavery; Spanish promise of freedom to runaways.
📌 Effects
Stricter slave codes (Negro Act of 1740) – prohibited assembly, education, earning money; reduced enslaved people's autonomy.
🟠 PERIOD 2
17. First Great Awakening (1730s–1770s)
📖 Definition
Religious revival emphasizing emotional, personal faith. Key figures: Jonathan Edwards, George Whitefield. Old Lights vs New Lights.
⚡ Causes
Reaction against dry, intellectual Enlightenment rationalism; desire for emotional religious experience.
📌 Effects
Undermined established church authority; encouraged individualism and questioning of authority; fostered shared colonial identity; increased denominations.
🟠 PERIOD 2
18. Enlightenment
📖 Definition
Intellectual movement emphasizing reason, science, natural rights (John Locke: life, liberty, property). Spread ideas about liberty, self-government, social contract.
⚡ Causes
Scientific Revolution; European philosophers (Locke, Rousseau, Montesquieu).
📌 Effects
Provided philosophical foundation for American Revolution; undermined monarchy; influenced Declaration of Independence and Constitution.
🟠 PERIOD 2
19. Navigation Acts (1651–1730s)
📖 Definition
British laws restricting colonial trade to English ships and enumerated goods. Mercantilism – colonies exist for mother country's benefit.
⚡ Causes
British desire to control colonial trade and maximize wealth.
📌 Effects
Limited colonial economic freedom; smuggling; salutary neglect allowed autonomy; after 1763 stricter enforcement sparked resistance.
🟡 PERIOD 3 (1754–1800)
20. French and Indian War / Seven Years' War (1754–1763)
📖 Definition
Britain (+ colonists) vs. France (+ Indian allies) over Ohio River Valley. Treaty of Paris (1763): Britain gained French Canada & land east of Mississippi.
⚡ Causes
Competing claims to Ohio Valley; colonists wanted to expand west.
📌 Effects
British massive debt → end of salutary neglect → new taxes; Proclamation of 1763; Pontiac's Rebellion.
🟡 PERIOD 3
21. Proclamation of 1763
📖 Definition
Forbade colonial settlement west of the Appalachian Mountains. Reserved land for Native Americans.
⚡ Causes
British desire to avoid further costly wars with Natives after Pontiac's Rebellion.
📌 Effects
Angered colonists who fought for western land; first major post-war grievance; many colonists ignored it.
🟡 PERIOD 3
22. Stamp Act (1765) & Colonial Resistance
📖 Definition
First direct tax on colonists – required stamps on printed goods. Violent protests, boycotts, Stamp Act Congress, Sons of Liberty.
⚡ Causes
British need for revenue after war debt.
📌 Effects
Parliament repealed Stamp Act but passed Declaratory Act; unified colonial opposition; "no taxation without representation."
🟡 PERIOD 3
23. Boston Tea Party / Intolerable Acts (1773–1774)
📖 Definition
Tea Act (1773) gave British East India monopoly → Boston Tea Party (colonists dumped tea). Intolerable Acts closed Boston Harbor, revoked MA charter, quartering.
⚡ Causes
British assertion of tax authority; colonial resistance.
📌 Effects
First Continental Congress (1774); colonial militias formed; path to revolution.
🟡 PERIOD 3
24. Declaration of Independence (July 4, 1776)
📖 Definition
Written by Thomas Jefferson, based on John Locke's natural rights (life, liberty, pursuit of happiness). List of grievances against King George III.
⚡ Causes
British tyranny; Enlightenment ideas; colonial unity after fighting began.
📌 Effects
Formalized revolution; inspired other movements; established American principles.
🟡 PERIOD 3
25. Articles of Confederation (1781–1789)
📖 Definition
First national government. Weak: no power to tax, no executive/judicial, 9/13 to pass laws, unanimous to amend. Accomplishments: won Revolutionary War, Northwest Ordinance (1787).
⚡ Causes
Fear of strong central government like British monarchy.
📌 Effects
Shays' Rebellion (1786-87) exposed weakness → led to Constitutional Convention (1787).
🟡 PERIOD 3
26. Constitutional Convention (1787) & Compromises
📖 Definition
Great Compromise: House by population, Senate equal. 3/5 Compromise: enslaved counted as 3/5. Slave trade protected for 20 years. Fugitive Slave Clause.
⚡ Causes
AOC failures; Shays' Rebellion; need for stronger national government.
📌 Effects
Created U.S. Constitution (ratified 1788); Bill of Rights added 1791; separation of powers, checks and balances.
🟡 PERIOD 3
27. Bill of Rights (1791)
📖 Definition
First 10 amendments – secured Anti-Federalist support for Constitution. Protected individual liberties (speech, religion, press, trial by jury, etc.).
⚡ Causes
Anti-Federalist demand for protections against federal power.
📌 Effects
Limited federal power; cornerstone of American civil liberties.
🟡 PERIOD 3
28. Washington's Farewell Address (1796)
📖 Definition
Warned against 1) political parties (factions) and 2) permanent foreign alliances.
⚡ Causes
Washington's two-term precedent; desire to guide future policy.
📌 Effects
Influenced U.S. isolationist policy for ~150 years; warning against parties proved prophetic.
🟡 PERIOD 3
29. Alien and Sedition Acts (1798) & KY-VA Resolutions
📖 Definition
Alien & Sedition Acts restricted immigrants, criminalized criticism of government. KY and VA Resolutions (Jefferson/Madison) argued states could nullify unconstitutional laws.
⚡ Causes
Federalist fear of foreign influence; French revolutionary radicalism.
📌 Effects
Backlash led to Jefferson's election (1800); nullification precedent for states' rights.
🟡 PERIOD 3
30. Revolution of 1800 (Election of 1800)
📖 Definition
Jefferson (Democratic-Republican) defeated incumbent Adams (Federalist). First peaceful transfer of power between opposing parties in modern history.
⚡ Causes
Backlash against Alien and Sedition Acts; Federalist division.
📌 Effects
Established peaceful transition via election; marked decline of Federalists; led to 12th Amendment.
🟢 PERIOD 4 (1800–1848)
31. Louisiana Purchase (1803)
📖 Definition
Jefferson bought from France for $15 million – doubled U.S. size. Jefferson used loose construction (ironic).
⚡ Causes
Napoleon needed money for European wars; U.S. wanted control of New Orleans.
📌 Effects
Opened vast western lands; fueled westward expansion; exacerbated slavery debate (Missouri Compromise later).
🟢 PERIOD 4
32. Marbury v. Madison (1803)
📖 Definition
Supreme Court under John Marshall established judicial review – Court can declare laws unconstitutional.
⚡ Causes
Adams's "midnight judges"; Jefferson refused to deliver Marbury's commission.
📌 Effects
Established Supreme Court's power to strike down laws; cornerstone of judicial authority.
🟢 PERIOD 4
33. Missouri Compromise (1820)
📖 Definition
Missouri admitted as slave state, Maine as free state. 36°30' line banned slavery north of it in Louisiana Territory (except Missouri).
⚡ Causes
Dispute over slavery expansion into Louisiana Purchase; need to maintain Senate balance.
📌 Effects
Temporarily resolved sectional conflict (lasted 34 years); postponed Civil War; precedent for using geography to limit slavery.
🟢 PERIOD 4
34. Jacksonian Democracy / Indian Removal Act (1830)
📖 Definition
"Common Man" president, spoils system. Indian Removal Act forced tribes west → Trail of Tears (1838): 4,000 Cherokee died. Jackson ignored Worcester v. Georgia.
⚡ Causes
Expansion of democracy; desire for southern land (gold); Jackson's hostility to Natives.
📌 Effects
Strengthened executive power; devastated Native nations; led to second party system (Democrats vs Whigs).
🟢 PERIOD 4
35. Nullification Crisis (1832–33)
📖 Definition
South Carolina declared tariffs null. John C. Calhoun argued nullification. Jackson threatened force (Force Bill). Compromise tariff ended crisis.
⚡ Causes
Southern opposition to "Tariff of Abominations" (1828) which hurt southern economy.
📌 Effects
Showed federal supremacy; previewed secession argument (states' rights vs. federal power).
🟢 PERIOD 4
36. Market Revolution & Reform Movements
📖 Definition
Shift to commercial economy: Erie Canal, railroads, telegraph, interchangeable parts. Second Great Awakening inspired temperance, abolition (Garrison, Douglass), women's rights (Seneca Falls 1848).
⚡ Causes
Industrialization; innovation; religious revival; reform spirit.
📌 Effects
Middle class, Cult of Domesticity, Irish/German immigration, wage labor. Declaration of Sentiments (1848) launched women's suffrage movement.
🔵 PERIOD 5 (1844–1877)
37. Manifest Destiny & Mexican-American War (1846–48)
📖 Definition
Belief in God-given right to expand. Mexican Cession (CA, NM, AZ, UT, NV) via Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo.
⚡ Causes
Border dispute; President Polk provoked Mexico; Manifest Destiny.
📌 Effects
Massive land acquisition; reignited slavery debate (Wilmot Proviso); Thoreau's "Civil Disobedience."
🔵 PERIOD 5
38. Compromise of 1850 & Fugitive Slave Law
📖 Definition
CA free state; popular sovereignty in UT/NM; stricter Fugitive Slave Law; ended DC slave trade. Fugitive Slave Law required citizens to help capture runaways.
⚡ Causes
Debate over slavery in Mexican Cession; CA Gold Rush.
📌 Effects
Fugitive Slave Law radicalized Northerners; increased Underground Railroad; temporary delay of secession.
🔵 PERIOD 5
39. Kansas-Nebraska Act (1854) & Bleeding Kansas
📖 Definition
Applied popular sovereignty to KS/NB, repealing Missouri Compromise line. Led to "Bleeding Kansas" – pro vs anti-slavery violence (John Brown, Pottawatomie).
⚡ Causes
Stephen Douglas wanted transcontinental railroad through Chicago; needed Southern support.
📌 Effects
Destroyed Whig Party; created Republican Party; previewed Civil War violence.
🔵 PERIOD 5
40. Dred Scott v. Sandford (1857)
📖 Definition
Supreme Court ruled: 1) Blacks not citizens; 2) Slaves are property; 3) Congress cannot ban slavery in territories (MO Compromise unconstitutional).
⚡ Causes
Dred Scott sued for freedom after living in free territories; Chief Justice Taney (pro-slavery).
📌 Effects
Outraged the North; made slavery legal in all territories; pushed nation closer to war.
🔵 PERIOD 5
41. Election of 1860 & Secession
📖 Definition
Lincoln (Republican) won with no Southern electoral votes. Free-soil platform. South Carolina seceded Dec 1860; 7 states formed Confederacy.
⚡ Causes
Democratic Party split; Lincoln's anti-slavery expansion stance.
📌 Effects
Civil War began at Fort Sumter (April 1861).
🔵 PERIOD 5
42. Emancipation Proclamation (1863)
📖 Definition
Freed slaves ONLY in areas of rebellion (not Border States). Changed war goal to ending slavery. Allowed Black enlistment (180,000+ served).
⚡ Causes
Military necessity; keep Europe from aiding Confederacy.
📌 Effects
Civil War became moral crusade; paved way for 13th Amendment.
🔵 PERIOD 5
43. Reconstruction (1865–1877) & Amendments
📖 Definition
13th (abolished slavery), 14th (citizenship, equal protection), 15th (Black male suffrage). Radical Reconstruction: military districts, required 14th Amendment. Freedmen's Bureau, Black Codes, sharecropping, KKK violence. Compromise of 1877 ended Reconstruction.
⚡ Causes
Need to readmit Southern states; status of freed people.
📌 Effects
Jim Crow segregation; Plessy v. Ferguson (1896) "separate but equal"; federal power expanded.
🟣 PERIOD 6 (1865–1898)
44. Gilded Age / Industrial Titans & Labor
📖 Definition
Rapid industrial expansion, inequality. Carnegie (vertical integration, Gospel of Wealth). Rockefeller (horizontal integration, Standard Oil). Social Darwinism. Knights of Labor, AFL (Gompers). Haymarket Riot (1886), Homestead (1892), Pullman (1894).
⚡ Causes
Technological change (Bessemer process, electricity); laissez-faire; railroads.
📌 Effects
Massive wealth gap; rise of labor unions; Sherman Antitrust Act (1890).
🟣 PERIOD 6
45. West & Native American Policies
📖 Definition
Homestead Act (1862), Transcontinental Railroad (1869), Dawes Act (1887) broke up tribal lands. Indian Wars: Sand Creek (1864), Little Bighorn (1876), Wounded Knee (1890). Chinese Exclusion Act (1882).
⚡ Causes
Manifest Destiny; gold/silver rushes; desire for land and assimilation.
📌 Effects
Native American land loss and cultural destruction; frontier closed (1890); nativist immigration restrictions.
🟣 PERIOD 6
46. Populist Party & Plessy v. Ferguson (1896)
📖 Definition
Populist Omaha Platform: direct election of senators (17th Am.), free silver, income tax, gov't ownership of railroads. Plessy v. Ferguson upheld "separate but equal" – legalized Jim Crow.
⚡ Causes
Farmer debt; railroad abuses; Southern segregation laws.
📌 Effects
Populist dissolved but reforms adopted later (Progressive Era). Plessy allowed segregation until 1954.
⚪ PERIOD 7 (1890–1945)
47. American Imperialism (Spanish-American War 1898)
📖 Definition
U.S. defeated Spain; gained Guam, Puerto Rico, Philippines (paid $20M). Platt Amendment made Cuba a U.S. protectorate. Open Door Policy (China). Roosevelt Corollary (1904) – U.S. police power in W. Hemisphere.
⚡ Causes
Yellow journalism; De Lôme Letter; USS Maine explosion.
📌 Effects
U.S. emerged as world power; Philippine-American War (1899–1902); anti-imperialist debate.
⚪ PERIOD 7
48. Progressive Era Reforms
📖 Definition
Muckrakers (Sinclair, Tarbell). 16th (income tax), 17th (direct election of senators), 18th (Prohibition), 19th (women's suffrage). Clayton Antitrust, Federal Reserve Act (1913).
⚡ Causes
Gilded Age abuses; corruption; industrialization problems.
📌 Effects
Expanded federal regulation; modern banking system; direct democracy measures.
⚪ PERIOD 7
49. World War I & Home Front
📖 Definition
U.S. entered 1917 due to Lusitania (1915), Zimmermann Telegram. Espionage & Sedition Acts, Schenck v. U.S. (clear & present danger). Wilson's Fourteen Points, League of Nations (Senate rejected). Great Migration.
⚡ Causes
Unrestricted submarine warfare; German aggression.
📌 Effects
U.S. isolationism after WWI; Red Scare (Palmer Raids); quota acts (1921, 1924).
⚪ PERIOD 7
50. Great Depression & New Deal
📖 Definition
Black Tuesday (1929); bank failures; Smoot-Hawley Tariff. New Deal programs: FDIC, SEC, CCC, WPA, TVA, AAA, Social Security Act (1935), Wagner Act, Fair Labor Standards Act (1938).
⚡ Causes
Stock speculation; overproduction; weak banks; drought.
📌 Effects
Created modern safety net; New Deal Coalition; WWII ended Depression.
⚪ PERIOD 7
51. World War II & Home Front
📖 Definition
Pearl Harbor (Dec 7, 1941). Japanese Internment (EO 9066 – Korematsu v U.S.). Double V Campaign, Rosie the Riveter. D-Day (1944). Atomic bombs (Hiroshima/Nagasaki, Aug 1945).
⚡ Causes
Japanese expansion; U.S. embargo; Nazi aggression.
📌 Effects
U.S. became superpower; United Nations; beginning of Cold War.
